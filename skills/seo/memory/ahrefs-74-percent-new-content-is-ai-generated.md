## Core Concept
Ahrefs’ study shows AI-assisted publishing is now the norm at web scale, which means content abundance is no longer a competitive advantage by itself.

## Key Takeaways
- Most newly published pages now include some AI-generated content.
- Pure human writing is the minority, which means “using AI” is not a differentiator anymore.
- Since AI assistance is now normal, the winning edge shifts to editorial judgment, expertise, structure, and usefulness.
- The study also undermines simplistic anti-AI narratives: AI use alone does not define quality or rankings.
- Content competition is now a volume and sameness problem, not just a keyword problem.
- This is one of the strongest background memories for why the squad must prioritize brand, evidence, topical depth, and post-click satisfaction.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Content saturation model**: AI lowers the cost of publishing, increasing sameness
- **Differentiation shift**: advantage moves from production speed to editorial quality and uniqueness
- **Human-plus-AI model**: mixed workflows are becoming the default, not the exception

## Data/Stats
- Published: May 19, 2025
- Sample: 900,000 newly created English-language web pages
- Findings:
  - 74.2% contained AI-generated content
  - 2.5% were categorized as pure AI
  - 25.8% were categorized as pure human
  - 71.7% were categorized as mixed human and AI

## How The Team Should Use This
- **Plankton (Writer)**: Focus on differentiation, examples, and sharper thinking rather than resisting AI in principle.
- **Coral (SEO)**: Stop treating AI usage as the risk; treat low-value sameness as the risk.
- **Pinchy (Orchestrator)**: Build content processes that combine efficiency with quality control, not one or the other.
- **Current (Marketing)**: Invest in brand and proof because commodity content will be increasingly interchangeable.
- **Key insight for @learnaiwithvijay**: AI content is normal now. The moat is no longer production. It is judgment.
