## Core Concept
Ahrefs shows that AI brand visibility cannot be judged by citation counts alone because brands are mentioned far more often than they are linked, and high-volume prompts can make low citation rates still highly visible.

## Key Takeaways
- Mentions and citations are different things, and both matter.
- AI assistants mention brands frequently without linking to them, which means raw referral traffic understates actual exposure.
- Ahrefs’ own brand was linked only around 28% of the time on average across tracked AI systems.
- Platform differences are large. AI Overviews were the stingiest while Perplexity linked much more often.
- Impressions matter more than citation rate alone because links on high-volume prompts can create outsized visibility.
- This note is strategically important because it changes how the squad should measure AI search wins.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Mentions vs citations vs impressions**: separate brand naming, link inclusion, and visibility-weighted exposure
- **Volume-weighted visibility model**: search demand is used as a proxy for prompt popularity
- **Citation-rate benchmarking**: compare platforms rather than assuming one global link rate

## Data/Stats
- Published: November 26, 2025
- Source dataset: 31,000+ Ahrefs brand mentions pulled from a 150M-prompt Brand Radar database
- Average link rate across six AI platforms: about 28%
- Platform rates highlighted by Ahrefs:
  - AI Overviews: 10.7%
  - Gemini: 16.8%
  - Copilot: 26.1%
  - ChatGPT: 26.9%
  - AI Mode: 36.8%
  - Perplexity: 51.6%

- **Coral (SEO)**: Optimize for being mentioned on important prompts even when links are unlikely.
