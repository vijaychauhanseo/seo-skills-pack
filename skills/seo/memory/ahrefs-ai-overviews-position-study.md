## Core Concept
Ahrefs found that while AI Overviews usually appear in the top position, a meaningful minority appear lower in the SERP, which means AIOs should be treated as variable layout elements rather than fixed rank-1 features.

## Key Takeaways
- AI Overviews are not permanently locked to position 1.
- About 8.64% of observed AIOs appeared outside the first position, as low as position 6.
- This suggests Google may be scoring when and where AIOs deserve prominence instead of forcing them above all results every time.
- SERP layout volatility matters more now because both organic links and AI blocks can shift relative prominence by query and country.
- Teams should not assume AIO behavior is static across markets or query types.
- This article strengthens the case for screenshot-level SERP inspection and feature-aware forecasting, not just keyword rank tracking.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **AIO placement model**: treat AIOs as variable-position SERP features
- **Market variance model**: track country-level differences in AI SERP behavior
- **Visibility shift model**: rank alone is not a stable proxy for screen prominence

## Data/Stats
- Published: July 24, 2025
- Sample: 10M SERPs with AI Overviews, 1M per country across 10 countries
- Finding: 91.36% of AIOs appeared in position 1, while 8.64% appeared lower, down to position 6
