## Core Concept
Ahrefs updated its earlier AIO CTR research and found that the impact appears materially worse: by December 2025, AI Overviews correlated with a 58% lower CTR for the top-ranking result.

## Key Takeaways
- The first wave of AIO CTR loss was severe; the updated wave is worse.
- Ahrefs re-ran the same methodology and found the top-ranking page now loses far more clicks when an AI Overview is present.
- This changes forecasting. Ranking number one is no longer enough to assume strong click capture on informational queries.
- Informational content economics are under pressure, which means teams need to value assisted awareness, citations, and branded demand more explicitly.
- The article strengthens the case for moving from “traffic only” reporting to visibility + demand + conversion reporting.
- For agents, this is a high-priority strategic memory because it changes how SEO wins should be measured and prioritized.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **AIO CTR benchmark update**: repeat prior study with newer data
- **Like-for-like comparison design**: compare AIO-triggering informational keywords against similar non-AIO informational keywords
- **Traffic compression model**: SERP visibility can rise while click opportunity shrinks

## Data/Stats
- Published: February 4, 2026
- Sample: 300,000 keywords
- Finding: AI Overviews correlate with a 58% lower average CTR for position 1
- Ahrefs reports position 1 CTR for AIO keywords falling to 0.016 in December 2025 versus an estimated 0.037 without AIO rollout

## How The Team Should Use This
- **Coral (SEO)**: Reprioritize content around topics where citations, product awareness, or downstream branded demand still justify the effort.
- **Pinchy (Orchestrator)**: Update reporting expectations and stakeholder communication. Rankings are no longer a clean proxy for traffic.
- **Plankton (Writer)**: Write stronger intros and clearer branded claims so even partial SERP exposure reinforces memory and trust.
- **Current (Marketing)**: Track branded search lift and conversion quality, not only sessions.
- **Charles**: Amplify content outside Google so the business is less dependent on shrinking informational CTR.
