## Core Concept
Ahrefs found that AI search traffic behaves differently from classic blog-driven SEO traffic: most of it goes to high-trust commercial and utility pages rather than informational articles.

## Key Takeaways
- AI search referral traffic is concentrated in a few page types, not evenly distributed across a site.
- Homepage, product pages, and free tools captured over 80% of Ahrefs’ measured AI search traffic.
- This suggests AI assistants often send users to pages with clear brand identity, product utility, or direct action paths.
- Blog content still matters because it builds the authority and retrieval layer, but it may not be the primary destination for AI-originated visits.
- Measurement is still incomplete. Ahrefs explicitly notes that AI traffic attribution is undercounted and AI Overviews traffic often remains mixed into standard organic.
- The strategic implication is important for the squad: SEO content strategy should support product, tool, and homepage discoverability, not only publish articles.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **AI traffic distribution model**: understand which page types capture AI referrals
- **Commercial destination bias**: AI assistants may prefer sending users to pages closest to action
- **Attribution caution model**: measured AI traffic is directionally useful but incomplete

## Data/Stats
- Published: June 18, 2025
- Ahrefs reports 0.5% of last-30-day traffic from AI search and 0.3% year-to-date in its own analytics
- Over 80% of measured AI search traffic went to homepage, product pages, and free tools

## How The Team Should Use This
- **Coral (SEO)**: Make sure strategic pages like homepage, tools, and product pages are optimized for entity clarity and retrieval, not only blog pages.
- **Pinchy (Orchestrator)**: Rebalance reporting so AI search wins are not judged only by blog-session growth.
- **Plankton (Writer)**: Write blog content that supports and links into the commercial and tool pages AI users are likely to visit next.
- **Current (Marketing)**: Use blog content to build demand, but expect conversion-oriented AI traffic to land elsewhere.
