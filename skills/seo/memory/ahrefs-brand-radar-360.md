## Core Concept
Ahrefs positions Brand Radar as a 360-degree brand intelligence system, combining AI visibility, search demand, web visibility, and video/community presence so teams can treat AI search as a measurable acquisition channel.

## Key Takeaways
- Brand Radar is designed to show how brands appear across AI responses, not just whether they rank in search.
- The core value is not one report. It is the combination of AI visibility, search demand, web visibility, and topic/category association.
- This turns AI search from a vague awareness surface into something that can be analyzed like a funnel input.
- Brand visibility work becomes cross-functional because SEO, brand, content, and distribution all affect the output.
- The article reinforces a major shift: the question is no longer “do we rank?” but “where are we discoverable across the systems that shape modern search?”
- For the squad, this is a foundational operating memory because it explains how multiple visibility surfaces connect.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **360-degree brand visibility model**: AI mentions + search demand + web mentions + topic association
- **Sales-channel framing**: treat AI visibility as commercially relevant, not just informational exposure
- **Topic-positioning model**: study which prompts and categories already connect to the brand
