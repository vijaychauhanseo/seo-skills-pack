## Core Concept
Ahrefs turns E-E-A-T from a vague principle into an operational audit system, arguing that brand credibility can be reviewed through 220+ concrete markers across the brand, people, pages, and site-wide trust layer.

## Key Takeaways
- E-E-A-T is not something you “add” with a few page tweaks. It is the result of many visible trust and authority markers working together over time.
- The Ahrefs checklist breaks the audit into tangible entities: brand and website foundations, key people, page-level signals, off-site reputation, technical trust, and AI visibility.
- AI search is now part of the audit. If your brand is not cited or mentioned in AI responses, that is a measurable trust and authority gap.
- Off-site reputation matters as much as on-site claims because Google quality systems rely heavily on independent corroboration.
- Technical trust still counts. Missing schema, security issues, broken links, or weak site hygiene undercut brand credibility even if content quality is strong.
- This article is strategically important because it translates abstract E-E-A-T talk into an executable checklist agents can run.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **220+ point E-E-A-T audit**: score brand, people, pages, and technical/site-wide trust
- **Entity-based audit model**: audit the company, the experts, and the content separately
- **AI visibility layer**: measure citations, mentions, and topic association in AI platforms
- **5-point rating method**: convert qualitative trust review into a shareable scorecard
