## Core Concept
This Ahrefs guide is the baseline operating manual for AI Overviews: where they show, how they behave, and why they materially change CTR, citation strategy, and informational SEO economics.

## Key Takeaways
- AI Overviews are no longer experimental edge cases. They are a growing surface that changes how informational SERPs work.
- AIOs appear disproportionately on informational, longer, and higher-volume queries, while showing less on branded and local searches.
- Citation concentration is real. A small set of domains captures a large share of mentions.
- AIOs reduce clicks while increasing visibility, making impression growth a less useful standalone KPI.
- Google keeps expanding AIO capabilities, coverage, and interface design, which means this surface will keep changing.
- Teams need a dedicated AIO model for content planning, traffic forecasting, and citation targeting.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **AIO surface model**: understand which query types trigger AIOs most often
- **Citation concentration model**: large domains dominate mentions unless others build stronger topical authority
- **Visibility-versus-clicks model**: impressions and rankings can improve while visits shrink

## Data/Stats
- Published: June 2025
- Ahrefs reports AIOs showing for 9.46% of desktop keywords overall and 16% in the U.S.
- AIOs were associated with a 34.5% reduction in clicks for the top organic result in the original study
- Ahrefs notes the top 50 domains captured 28.90% of all mentions in its 55.8M AIO study

## How The Team Should Use This
- **Coral (SEO)**: Forecast traffic conservatively on informational content with heavy AIO exposure.
- **Pinchy (Orchestrator)**: Separate citation goals from click goals in reporting and planning.
- **Plankton (Writer)**: Create citation-friendly sections that can survive even if clicks decline.
- **Current (Marketing)**: Use AIO presence as a brand-exposure layer, then reinforce demand through brand channels and remarketing paths.
- **Key insight for @learnaiwithvijay**: Winning the SERP no longer guarantees winning the click. The answer layer must be treated as its own channel.
