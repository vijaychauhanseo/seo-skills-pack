## Core Concept
This large Ahrefs AIO study establishes the baseline economics of AI Overviews: they are growing, highly concentrated, and disproportionately affect informational search visibility.

## Key Takeaways
- AI Overviews are not a niche experiment; they are a growing SERP layer with massive footprint.
- AIOs show more often on informational queries and where search volume is high, which puts classic top-of-funnel SEO under the most pressure.
- Citation concentration is significant, meaning a relatively small set of domains captures a large share of AIO mentions.
- The study supports a model where AIO presence and AI citation strategy must be monitored separately from traditional keyword rank.
- It also gives the squad scale confidence: this is not based on anecdotal screenshots but very large Ahrefs data.
- This note complements the CTR studies by explaining the larger surface dynamics behind them.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **AIO footprint model**: quantify how common AIOs are across the tracked keyword set
- **Visibility concentration model**: a small number of domains capture a disproportionate share of mentions
- **Informational-query pressure model**: top-of-funnel traffic is under the most AIO pressure

## Data/Stats
- Published: May 30, 2025
- Dataset: 55.8M AI Overviews across 590M searches
- Ahrefs reports AIOs showing for 9.46% of all tracked keywords and 12.8% or more of searches by volume
