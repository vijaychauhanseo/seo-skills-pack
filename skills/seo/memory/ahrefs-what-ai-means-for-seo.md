## Core Concept
Ahrefs’ position is clear: AI has changed search behavior and traffic economics, but the core disciplines of SEO are still the best way to win visibility across both search engines and AI assistants.

## Key Takeaways
- AI assistants are a real discovery layer, but they are not replacing Google. Users now search across both.
- SEO traffic is getting squeezed by zero-click behavior and AI Overviews, but that does not mean SEO is dead; it means the economics and KPIs are changing.
- Content creation is cheaper because of AI, which raises the competitive floor and increases the value of expertise and differentiation.
- AI visibility depends heavily on traditional search visibility because AI systems often ground responses in web search results.
- The familiar SEO pillars still matter: content creation, links and mentions, and technical accessibility.
- The article is valuable for agents because it stops the false choice between SEO and GEO and gives one operating model for both.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **SEO + GEO overlap model**: optimize once for both search engines and AI assistants
- **Three-pillar continuity**: content, links/mentions, and technical health remain foundational
- **Grounding logic**: AI assistants rely on search retrieval for many live and factual queries

## Data/Stats
- Published: January 19, 2026
- ChatGPT cited at 800M weekly active users
- Google yearly searches reportedly grew 22% even during peak AI adoption
- Ahrefs states over 20% of all U.S. keywords now show an AI Overview
