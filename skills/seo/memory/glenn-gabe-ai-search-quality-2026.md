# Glenn Gabe on AI Search Traffic, Core Quality Systems, and Feature Swaps

## Core Concept
Glenn Gabe is the best operator in this pack when the problem is quality-system sensitivity across Google surfaces. Use him when you need to connect core updates, AI visibility, and reporting blind spots into one diagnosis.

## Highest-Signal Takeaways
- Glenn's June 25, 2025 analysis found AI Search traffic was still less than 1% for most sites and under 0.5% for many. His main warning was not to overreact to AI-search hype by neglecting Google Search.
- He tied that directly to quality risk: if sites chase unproven AI-search tactics and let quality slip, they can get hit by broad core updates and lose visibility across traditional search and AI layers together.
- In his July 21, 2025 analysis of the June core update, Glenn highlighted that AI Overviews and AI Mode sit inside the `Web Search` bucket and are "rooted in Google's core quality and ranking systems." That makes core-update thinking directly relevant to AI visibility.
- That same June 2025 update analysis showed real AI Overview visibility loss for sites that were hit by the core update. For Glenn, the lesson is simple: AI surfaces do not float above Google's quality systems.
- Glenn also tracks SERP feature substitution. In his featured-snippet tracking analysis, he showed featured snippets falling sharply while AI Overviews rose, including examples of a 35% and 57% drop in featured-snippet-yielding queries between September 2024 and March 2025.
- He also noted AI Overview prevalence rising to nearly 19% of queries, while GSC still does not break out AIO and featured-snippet performance cleanly. That creates a serious measurement blind spot.

## What Glenn Adds To The Squad
- A quality-system-first explanation for AI visibility losses.
- Strong awareness that broad core updates can move AI surfaces, not just blue links.
- Practical feature-swap thinking: AIOs replacing or suppressing older SERP features.
- A healthy skepticism toward traffic narratives that outrun actual analytics.
