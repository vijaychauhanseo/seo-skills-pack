# Google Search Central on AI Features and Structured Data

## Core Concept
Google's current public position is consistent: AI features still sit on top of standard Search requirements. The priority is not special "AI SEO tricks", but technical eligibility, visible content, accurate markup, and helpful people-first pages.

## Highest-Signal Official Points
- AI features such as AI Overviews and AI Mode can surface relevant links and often expose searchers to a wider mix of sites for more complex tasks.
- Google says the same baseline SEO best practices apply to AI features as to Search overall: meet technical requirements, follow spam policies, and create helpful, reliable, people-first content.
- Structured data can support understanding and rich results, but it is not a shortcut. Markup must match visible page content and stay accurate and current.
- Invisible, misleading, or irrelevant markup can block rich-result eligibility and create trust risk.
- For AI visibility diagnostics, use official docs as the floor. If a page fails crawl, canonical, rendering, or markup basics, do not jump straight to model theories.

## Practical Reading of Google's Position
- Google has not published a separate official "AI ranking playbook". Treat AI surfaces as Search plus new rendering and selection layers.
- When Google discusses AI features, it still anchors on Search Essentials, technical accessibility, and user-first content.
- For the squad, that means AI visibility work starts with SEO eligibility, then moves into retrieval, selection, and answer-layer analysis.
