## Core Concept
Hobo presents `contentEffort` as a leaked Google quality signal that attempts to estimate how much real labour, expertise, and originality went into an article, making low-cost mass content structurally disadvantaged.

## Key Takeaways
- `contentEffort` is described as an LLM-based effort estimate for article pages, which implies Google is evaluating qualitative signals rather than relying on crude content heuristics.
- This pushes SEO economics away from content volume and toward defensible assets that are harder to replicate.
- Original data, custom media, expert contribution, and visible process all raise the perceived effort behind a page.
- The signal is framed as closely tied to Helpful Content logic: content made cheaply for search demand is easier to detect and easier to demote.
- Hobo treats E-E-A-T as an emergent system rather than one score. `contentEffort` contributes to expertise and trust rather than replacing them.
- User feedback still matters. Algorithmic effort estimates can be validated or challenged by click and satisfaction signals.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Effort-based quality model**: estimate labour and originality, not just readability
- **Replicability test**: if a competitor or AI can easily reproduce the page, the page likely has low effort
- **Helpful content alignment**: people-first pages should show investment, evidence, and differentiation
