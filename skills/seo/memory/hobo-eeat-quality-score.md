## Core Concept
Hobo’s model treats E-E-A-T as Google’s public doctrine, Q* as the underlying quality system, and `Site_Quality` as the practical output that shapes a domain’s ability to rank.

## Key Takeaways
- E-E-A-T is framed as a philosophy that Google operationalises through many measurable quality inputs rather than one visible score.
- Trust is treated as the center of the whole model, especially for YMYL topics where low trust can override other strengths.
- Q* is described as the quality engine, while compressed quality modules and site-level fields supply the machine-readable inputs.
- Site-wide authority and page-level quality are linked. Individual URLs inherit advantages and penalties from the host domain’s quality profile.
- Click behavior, encoded quality signals, and authority attributes appear to converge in one wider quality architecture.
- Hobo’s framing supports a practical SEO doctrine: quality, trust, and topical alignment should be engineered across the whole site, not bolted onto isolated pages.

## Frameworks/Methods Covered
- **Doctrine vs system model**: E-E-A-T is the public principle; Q* is the operational system
- **Site_Quality output model**: aggregate document and host signals into a domain-level quality position
- **Compressed quality signals**: critical per-doc and site-level scores available for fast preliminary scoring
