# Mike King and iPullRank on Relevance Engineering and AI Search

## Core Concept
Mike King is the strongest strategic operator in this pack for rethinking SEO as retrieval engineering. Use him when the work needs to move beyond rank tracking into embeddings, fan-out, passage retrieval, and semantic competition.

## Highest-Signal Takeaways
- Mike's AI-search framing is explicit: visibility is probabilistic because systems break prompts into synthetic variations, retrieve passages semantically, and assemble responses from weighted fragments.
- The `Probability in AI Search` article is one of the best current explanations of why ranking for one head term no longer guarantees inclusion in AI answers.
- His SEO Week 2025 keynote introduced `Relevance Engineering` as an operating system that blends AI, information retrieval, content strategy, digital PR, and UX.
- iPullRank's public positioning aligns with that same view: brands win AI search by engineering visibility across query fan-out, passage retrieval, embeddings, and synthesis instead of focusing only on blue-link rankings.
- The Search Engine Land award write-up around Mike is also useful because it concisely captures what his camp is contributing: Qforia, query fan-out testing, AI Mode breakdowns, and technical frameworks for AI search.

## What Mike Adds To The Squad
- A retrieval-and-semantics-first explanation of modern SEO.
- Strong alignment with DEJAN on fan-out, passage-level selection, and answer inclusion.
- Better language for explaining why AI visibility feels non-deterministic.
