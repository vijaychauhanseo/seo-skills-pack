# Patrick Stox on AI Search Data, Measurement, and Technical SEO

## Core Concept
Patrick Stox is the strongest operator in our library for AI-search measurement reality. Use his work when the question is not "what should be true?" but "what does the data actually show?"

## Highest-Signal Takeaways
- Patrick's February 11, 2026 work showed ChatGPT at roughly 12% of Google's search volume, while Google still sent about 190x more traffic to websites. That keeps AI-search growth in perspective.
- His February 11, 2026 GSC study showed anonymized queries made up 46.77% of website traffic in April 2025 across 22 billion clicks and 887,534 GSC properties. In practice, measurement is getting worse as queries get longer and rarer.
- His June 16, 2025 Ahrefs conversion study found AI-search visitors converted far better than traditional search visitors for Ahrefs: 0.5% of visits drove 12.1% of signups, with a 23x better conversion-per-visit rate.
- His July 17, 2025 AI Mode test found Google AI Mode output was good enough for Google to show to users but not good enough to rank organically. That is a clean reminder that answer-layer usefulness and rank-layer quality are not the same system.
- His July 18, 2025 SERP analysis found AI Overviews appeared outside position 1 in 8.64% of cases and as low as position 6, suggesting Google may score AI Overviews rather than pinning them permanently to the top.
- His July 8, 2025 mention study is useful for testing whether highly linked pages correlate with AI mentions, which makes him a good bridge between links, citations, and AI visibility.

## What Patrick Adds To The Squad
- A measurement-first AI-search lens.
- Strong caution about over-reading GSC or analytics data in the AI era.
- A practical distinction between traffic volume and traffic quality.
- A reminder that AI features and organic ranking can diverge sharply.
